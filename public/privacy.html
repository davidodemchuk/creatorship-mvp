Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 6, 2026

What we collect from creators

When you connect your TikTok account via OAuth, we collect:

  • TikTok display name and avatar URL
  • Follower count and video count
  • Public video metadata (e.g., titles, cover images, view counts) as needed to surface your content to brands
  • TikTok access token (stored securely) to refresh profile data and, where applicable, access public video lists

We do not access your TikTok password or private messages.

What we collect from brands

For brand accounts we collect:

  • Email address (for login and communications)
  • Brand name and TikTok Shop store name
  • Meta API credentials (access token, ad account ID, page ID) when you choose to connect them, stored encrypted or in secure storage

How we use your data

We use creator data to show your profile and videos to brands for licensing and deal offers. We use brand data to run your account, connect campaigns to Meta, and send service-related emails. We may use aggregated, non-identifying analytics to improve the product.

What we never do

  • We never sell your personal data to third parties.
  • We never post to your TikTok account on your behalf.

Third parties

We rely on third-party services that have their own privacy policies:

  • TikTok API — for creator OAuth and profile/video data
  • Meta (Facebook) API — for ad account and campaign operations when brands connect Meta
  • Stripe — for payment processing where applicable
  • Railway (or similar hosting) — for hosting our application and databases

Each provider governs their use of data under their own terms and policies.

Security

We use industry-standard practices to protect data in transit (HTTPS) and at rest. Access tokens and credentials are stored with restricted access. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure; we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Your rights

You may request access to, correction of, or deletion of your personal data where applicable. Creators can disconnect TikTok at any time in the app. Brands can delete their account in Settings. We will honor deletion requests subject to legal or legitimate retention needs (e.g., transaction records).

CCPA (California)

If you are a California resident, you have rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), including the right to know what personal information we collect, the right to delete, and the right to opt out of sale. We do not sell personal information. To exercise CCPA rights, contact us at the email below.
